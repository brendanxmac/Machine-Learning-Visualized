K Means (Clustering)

« Introduction K Nearest Neighbors »


Introduction


Type of Machine Learning:

Unsupervised- Data is not labeled

Usage:

Classification (Clustering) - Finding groups within the data


Example





In a Nutshell

Group data into 'K' many groups / clusters. In the example above, K = 3 since there are three groupings that the data is being assigned to.

Abstract Explanation

K Means can be thought of in terms of a 'K' students transfering to a different college. These 'K' students represent the centroids in this case. All of the other students who were already at the school represent all of the data points. As the new students meet the current students they are doing to evaluate close they are to everyone. Day by day they will begin to become closer to those who are nearest to them and vice versa. In this way, eventually they will find themselves settled into a place where they have settled into a friend group full of other students who they are closest to. This is demonstrated in the animations below.

The algorithm only has a few steps:

  • Determine the most suitable number for K | e.g. how many centroids (classifiers / groups) make sense?
  • Determine a method for where to place those centroids, either randomly generated or randomly selected
  • Then repeatdly run three steps in order to find groups within the data set
    • 1) Assign each data point to the centroid nearest to it
    • 2) Calculate the mean distance (Euclidean Distance) of all points assigned to each centroid
    • 3) Move the centroids to the coordinate that is closest to all datapoints that are currently assigned to it

K Means Clustering Algorithm

  • Input:
    • K (Number of clusters)
    • the data set

  • Setup - Randomly generate starting positions for the K many centriods

  • 1) Data Assignment: Each centroid represents a cluster, each datapoint is assigned to nearest centroid
  • 2) Centroid Update: Calculate mean distance data points assigned to a centroid's cluster, move centroid to mean distance
  • 3) Repeat Steps 1 and 2 until centroids no longer update

  • For example: If K = 3 for a certain data set
