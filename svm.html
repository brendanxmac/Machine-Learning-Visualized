SVM (Support Vector Machine)

« K Nearest Neighbors Linear Regression »

Introduction


Learning Type: Unsupervised
Usage:Classification & Regression

In a Nutshell

Support vector machine is mostly a classification algorithm which means it is used to put data into different groups depending on their properties. Once all data is plotted on a graph, we use regression to determine an equation ( called a hyper-plane ) that will separate the data to the best of its ability. In otherwords, it looks at plotted data and tries to find an 'equation of separation' that will separate different groupings of data if those groups actually exist.

Abstract Explanation

To picture what this might look like, think about two different fenced in farms owned by the same person separated by a long road. On the left side of the road is farm one which contains dairy cows. On the right side of the road is farm b which contains beef cows. The owner of these farms has decided to split the cows up into groups, meaning they are different due to some classification. Now imagine that the greater the difference between these cows, the wider the road is. If the cows were extremely different for one reason or another, the road would be have many more lanes. If the cows weren't truly that different, the road would have one lane, or, in extreme cases, there might just be the fences that separate the cows.

In this example, the width of the road represents the value of the difference between the data point groups. The road is the split in the data and the correlation between the two groups. In some cases, imagine we have a smaller subgroup of cows that have a circular fence, and they are surrounded by a larger fenced in farm. This type of classification can be found within a dataset as well.
